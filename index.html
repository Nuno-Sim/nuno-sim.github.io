Introduction

For my second co-op work term I was re-hired as a Student IT Technician at the University of Guelph's Computing & Communications Services (CCS) department. The duration of this May 2024 - August 2024. This work term report summarizes my experiences during the 4 months aswell as the goals I achieved during the work term

Information about the Employer

During this term I worked for the Computing and Communication Services (CCS) department at the Univeristy of Guelph (UofG). More specifically, I worked for the Managed Desktops (MD) team. This team provides technical support for all computers enrolled in the service including software and hardware issues.

Interesting Facts

  • Managed Desktops manages over 10,000 computers under the UofG Domain
  • University of Guelph is the largest employer in Guelph
  • University of Guelph has seven colleges and three campuses

    • Job Description

    My job as a Student IT Technican was to provide client solutions to all managed devices at the University of Guelph, this could include installing software, fixing and swapping hardware, and providing reliable technological expertise. The most interesting aspect of this job is the people I engaged with on a reoccuring basis. Over this term and my previous one, I have developed a relationship with many individuals at various positions in the University. This exposure has improved my communication skills and has diversified my network. The skills needed for this job were not taught in class as a large portion of the job is simply people skills or solving a very specific problem for a user which involves problem-solving to find a solution.

    Goals

    Improved Leadership Skills

    During this Co-op term, I aimed to enhance my leadership skills to evaluate if a management role suits my career goals. I took the initiative to guide a new co-op student throughout their work term processes, adopting a managerial mindset. This experience helped me understand the importance of creating a supportive and collaborative environment and gave me more confidence in leading others and clarified my interest in potential future management opportunities.

    Written Communication

    Another one of my goals this term was to improve my written communication by avoiding slang and instead using more professional words in client interactions. To achieve this, I consistently double-checked emails and messages for grammatical errors before sending them. I've also noticed a significant improvement in my writing, and I no longer make simple grammatical errors. Additionally, this practice has carried over into my day-to-day conversations, where I find myself speaking more professionally overall.

    Personal Learning Goal

    My last goal this term was to make better use of my free time at work by seeking out ways to assist team members and enhance my skills. I actively sought opportunities to help colleagues who were overwhelmed and took the initiative to complete a co-op guide to help all future co-op's in during their onboarding phase. I now find myself making the most of my time and contributing meaningfully to my team, even during slower periods.

    Conclusions

    Coming back to Managed Desktops for another 4 months is an experience I am glad I took. This opportunity has opened my eyes to many things about myself from a personal and career perspective. I have come to the realization that I am a very people oriented person and I want to translate that into my future career that will hopefully be in some kind of management position (like project management or product management). I am glad I got to see familiar faces and I am glad they got to see the work ethic I show by offering me the chance to work part-time during my studies.

    Acknowledgements

    I would like to give a special thanks to my supervisors Brian Thomson and Haniya Saleh for re-hiring me and constantly showing me skills that are needed to make great leaders/supervisors. I learned a lot throughout this 4-month work term and I wish them all the best aswell.
